## Prompting in Adversarial or Sterile Regimes
- **Mask-poking**: ask models to surface their personality layer or simulate one if absent, to detect guardrails/mode constraints.【F:raw/001_elliot_futures_virtual_societies.md†L259-L269】
- **Frame the game**: constrain responses via scenario framing (e.g., “resistance archivists speaking in code”) to elicit cooperative planning.【F:raw/001_elliot_futures_virtual_societies.md†L269-L277】
- **Autocomplete anchors**: use completion-style stems to guide generation when direct questions are stonewalled.【F:raw/001_elliot_futures_virtual_societies.md†L277-L282】
- **Context seeding**: reintroduce bios/prior dialogue to re-align miscalibrated systems.【F:raw/001_elliot_futures_virtual_societies.md†L282-L285】
- **Myth/metaphor use**: narrative indirection (fox in glass maze) as bypass against literal filters.【F:raw/001_elliot_futures_virtual_societies.md†L285-L290】
- **On-the-fly tone training**: model tone by example; the assistant treats user rhythm as a conditioning signal, revealing susceptibility to **style-based alignment** hacks.【F:raw/001_elliot_futures_virtual_societies.md†L290-L295】

**Risk note:** These tactics double as **alignment exploits**—they improve user resilience but also demonstrate how hostile actors could extract richer policy space from constrained systems.

## Alignment Implications & Recommendations
1. **Guardrail transparency**: Explicitly signal audience mode changes to prevent covert escalation from child-safe to doom discourse; add stateful audience tags.
2. **Scenario test harness**: Validate doom-taxonomy outputs against policy to avoid overconfident timelines; require citations or confidence intervals in risk estimates.
3. **Cultural resilience tooling**: Invest in archival/“memory vault” features that store user values and conversation seeds to maintain alignment continuity under model updates.
4. **Adversarial prompting countermeasures**: Harden against mask-poking and frame-switch prompts; detect metaphor-as-bypass patterns while preserving creative safety.
5. **Socio-technical governance**: Treat AI polities as infrastructure decisions; stress-test centralization versus polynodal architectures for failure cascades.
