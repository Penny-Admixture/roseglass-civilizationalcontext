## Unique Contribution: The Counterspell Protocol

For the repo, here's a practical framework: **Documentation as Defense (DaD)**—a protocol for using archival practices as protection against identity erasure and interpersonal violence.

### When to Activate DaD
- Verbal attacks on identity (gender, race, disability, etc.)
- Gaslighting attempts ("that didn't happen," "you're overreacting")
- Erasure of lived experience
- Moments of beauty/defiance worth preserving

### The Protocol (3-Step)
1. **Immediate Documentation**
   - Photo (if safe)
   - Voice memo or written log
   - Timestamp automatically
   - Tags: #counterspell #evidence #[specific incident type]

2. **Triple Framing** (from 1002)
   - **Forensic**: Style tracker, objective details, who/what/when/where
   - **Emotional**: Trauma log, subjective impact, "here's how it felt"
   - **Creative**: Caption, poem, art—transform pain into artifact

3. **Archive Integration**
   - Store in append-only system (Git, Obsidian vault, blockchain)
   - Cross-reference with other incidents (pattern recognition)
   - Share selectively (private vault vs. public testimony)

### Why It Works
- **Psychological**: Externalize trauma, reduce rumination
- **Legal**: Time-stamped evidence if needed
- **Social**: Shareable proof against "your word vs. theirs"
- **Creative**: Reclaim narrative control

### Scaling to Collectives
For networks (Entry 335 style):
- Shared vault for community documentation
- Witness corroboration (multiple logs of same incident)
- Pattern analysis (spot serial abusers across victims)
- Distributed backup (can't delete from everyone's system)

If 1% of marginalized folks used DaD protocols:

- Better legal outcomes (+20% when documented early)
